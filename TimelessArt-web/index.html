Pre-colonial Art Period (6185 to 4400 BC)

Believed to be the age of the Horticulture/Neolithic period, local communities established art that went beyond mere craft. Pre-colonial traditional art featured religious symbols, everyday activities, and specific decorative patterns influenced by local animistic or Islamic beliefs. Art exchanges with Chinese and Asian traders influenced pottery, weaving, tattoo, jewelry, carving, and metal craft.

c. 890-710 BC
Manunggul Jar Artisans
Neolithic Pottery
Created one of the most significant archaeological finds in the Philippines. The Manunggul Jar shows sophisticated artistry and deep spiritual beliefs through its intricate design and boat-of-the-dead motif.
c. 3000 - 2000 BC
Angono Petroglyphs
Rock Art
The oldest known artwork in the Philippines, these rock carvings feature human and animal figures believed to be carved between 3000 BC and 2000 BC.
c. 15th Century
Bulul Wooden Figures
Wooden Figures
Carved wooden figures representing the Ifugao rice deity, symbolizing fertility and prosperity. These figures are often used in rituals and ceremonies.

Spanish Colonial Art Period (1521-1898)

Filipinos were introduced to formal painting, sculpture, and architecture inspired by Byzantine, Gothic, Baroque, and Rococo art. Religious (Catholic) themes dominated artworks. The elite Filipino class studying abroad brought influences of Neo-Classicism, Romanticism, and hints of impressionism.

1796 - 1834
Damián Domingo
Academic Realism
Pioneer of Philippine art education and founder of the first Philippine art academy. Known for his detailed watercolor paintings of Filipino types and costumes.
1855 - 1913
Félix Resurrección Hidalgo
Academic Art
One of the great Filipino painters of the late 19th century. Known for his historical pieces and mastery of light and shadow. His painting "El Asesinato del Gobernador Bustamante" depicts a dramatic moment in Philippine history.
1855 - 1914
Juan Luna
Romanticism
A prominent figure in Philippine art, known for his historical and allegorical paintings. His work "Spoliarium" won a gold medal at the 1884 Madrid Exposition.

American Colonial Art Period (1898-1946)

American influence brought education and value formation following the "American way of life". Art illustration, advertising, and commercial design gained popularity. Painting themes favored genre paintings, landscapes, and still life, while portraits maintained academic formality for high-ranking officials.

1892 - 1972
Fernando Amorsolo
Classical Realism
First National Artist of the Philippines, captured the bombing of Intendencia during WWII, showing his versatility beyond pastoral scenes.
1904 - 1967
Dominador Castañeda
Impressionism
Known for his seascapes and landscapes that captured the Filipino coastal life with impressionistic techniques.
1879 - 1946
Jorge Pineda
Realism
A pioneering editorial cartoonist and illustrator who helped establish political cartooning in the Philippines. His work "A Young Nation" reflects the country's aspirations during the American colonial period.

Post-Colonial Art Period (1946-1986)

Post-war art experienced significant growth and expansion, divided into Philippine Modern Art (1946-1970) and Philippine Post-Modern Art (1970-1980s). The latter period saw Pop Art, Installation Art, and Performance Art dominating the scene.

1912 – 1969
Carlos Francisco
Modernism
Also known as Botong Francisco, he revitalized the art of mural painting in the Philippines. His works, like "Bayanihan," reflect Filipino heritage, folklore, and nationalism through dynamic compositions and vivid storytelling.
1924 – 1984
Fernando Zóbel
Abstract Expressionism
A Spanish-Filipino painter known for his lyrical abstractions, Zóbel explored movement and light through gestural lines and refined color palettes. His painting "Noche Clara" exemplifies his intellectual and spiritual approach to art.
1931 – 1995
Jose Joya
Abstract Expressionism
A pioneering figure in modern Philippine art, Joya was known for his vibrant and dynamic abstractions inspired by the tropical landscape. His work "Grandean Arabesque" showcases bold brushwork and layered textures.

Contemporary Art Period (1980s to Present)

The rise of personal computers and new technology created new art mediums for expression. This period also saw revivals of old styles, creating a diverse artistic landscape that continues to evolve.

1924 - 1994
Nena Saguil
Abstract Art
Pioneer of abstract art in the Philippines, known for her cosmic and microscopic imagery that transcended traditional representation.
1937 - 2013
Roberto Chabet
Conceptual Art
Considered the father of Philippine Conceptual Art. His experimental works and installations challenged traditional art forms. He influenced generations of artists as a longtime professor at the University of the Philippines.
1910 - 1997
Tony Velasquez
Comic Art
Known as the Father of Philippine Comics. Created "Kenkoy," the first Filipino comic strip character. His work in Tagalog Klasiks helped establish the golden age of Philippine comics.
